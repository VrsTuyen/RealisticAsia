Environmentally Responsibility

Working in the travel industry means loving and appreciating the world we live in. Socially Responsibility

As customer demand for authentic experiences increases, the possibility of community exploitation grows as well. Realistic Asia seeks to appreciate and promote the diversity of the region’s cultures. This entails collaborating with local communities to promote their traditional culture and provide mutual benefit experiences. Equality and inclusiveness are crucial to the way we do business, from our day-to-day internal behavior to the partners we interact with, and by recognizing, practicing, and supporting these values, we make Realistic Asia a better place to work and a better organization to partner with.
Furthermore, we are constantly searching for innovative ways to empower and promote local businesses. We use local guides at each destination, who have the best knowledge about area and how to run the tour in the best way for the environment. In addition, we prefer the locally owned accommodations and offer our travelers the local cuisine meals. Thereby, we increase the amount of revenue that stays in local communities, as a result of this, which helps to promote development and income in underdeveloped areas.

  • We Leave free time in the itineraries and share local recommendations with clients so they can contribute to the local community.
  • We support and give preferences to local suppliers and local communities by purchasing locally produced goods and working with small enterprises.
  • We encourage the creation and suggest community based tours to our clients. This way we ensure the benefits go directly back into the community where we work and live.

Our Community Based Tourism Project

Bavi Eco Tour

As a big part of our contribution to community development, we have created our exclusive Ba Vi Eco Tour. The mission of this project is to give travelers deep knowledge about the daily life of Vietnamese villages, let them get closer to nature, and support the local community. For this, we have contributed to facilities development and offer an extra source of income for the hosts in the village. We do also limit the number of tours to this area to keep the balance between communication with guests and their main business to avoid any negative impact from tourism. We carefully select the activities and give the preference which let travelers explore the sites by cycling, trekking, or using a paddleboat, which is more eco-friendly.
